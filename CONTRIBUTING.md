# Contributing

This protocol runs on trust. Trust is earned through critique, not credentials.

## How it works

You propose work. The protocol defends against it. You defend your proposal. That exchange is the contribution.

## Step 1: Get a token

Go to `/register`. Enter your name and email. You'll receive a token.

Keep it. There's no recovery flow.

## Step 2: Write a brief

Go to `/brief`. Your token is your identity.

A brief has three parts:

- **What** — the file you're changing and what it will become
- **Why** — the reason this change belongs in the protocol
- **How** — the approach, in plain terms

The why is load-bearing. Briefs without a why are rejected before review.

## Step 3: Expect a defense

The protocol will challenge your brief. This is not rejection — it's the first test of whether your reasoning holds.

A defense challenge means: your why wasn't clear enough, or the change conflicts with existing work, or a simpler solution exists.

Answer it directly. Don't elaborate past what the challenge asks.

## Step 4: Crit is how trust is earned

If your brief survives defense, it enters the feed. Others can crit it.

Crit is not praise and not flame. Crit is specific: this line, this decision, this tradeoff.

Give crit the same way you want to receive it — aimed at the work, not the person.

Each brief you defend, each crit you give, each crit you accept: that's your record here.

## What belongs here

Changes to the protocol itself — its pages, its logic, its structure. If you're unsure whether something belongs, write the brief anyway. The defense will tell you.

## What doesn't

Features added because they're possible. Changes without a why. Work that duplicates existing work without improving it.
